UK slip road — left turn, wet conditions
Original Facts
1. ❌ I am driving at 4 mph (20 km/h) on a wet, two-lane slip road in the UK.
2. My lane is designated for turning left, as indicated by large white arrows on the road.

4. To my right is a multi-lane divided highway. A blue car is passing in the adjacent lane.
5. A circular sign indicates a 30 mph speed limit for the road I am leaving.
6. A rectangular sign below indicates the left turn is for 'Local traffic'.
7. The road I am turning onto is narrower, with 20 mph and 'No U-turn' signs visible.
8. A red double-decker bus is visible in the distance on the street I am entering.
9. ❌ A pedestrian is on the sidewalk to my left, near the traffic light pole.
10. ❌ To my left, there is a dedicated cycle lane marked with bicycle symbols.
11. ❌ Road surfaces are wet, indicating recent rainfall.
12. ❌ A BP gas station is visible on the right of the main highway.
Corrected Facts
1. ✅ I am driving at approximately 10–20 mph on a wet two-lane slip road in the UK.
2–8. [Accepted as-is]
9. ✅ A pedestrian is on the sidewalk to my right, near the traffic light pole.
10. ✅ There is a brick wall and residential housing along both sides of the new street.
11. ✅ Road surface is wet with scattered leaves along the curve; visibility is clear despite overcast sky.
12. ✅ A traffic cone is positioned near the right side of the junction.
Original Reasoning
1. The green traffic light indicates I have right of way to make the left turn.
2. ❌ My slow speed of 4 mph is appropriate for the sharp turn given wet conditions.
3. I must remain aware of the blue car passing on my right to ensure no conflict.
4. ❌ I need to monitor the pedestrian on the left to ensure they do not step into the road.
5. Upon completing the turn, I must prepare to adhere to the 20 mph speed limit.
6. ❌ The double-decker bus may stop and cause passengers to cross the road.
Corrected Reasoning
1. [Accepted]
2. ✅ My moderate speed is appropriate for the wet conditions and curvature of the turn.
3. [Accepted]
4. ✅ I need to remain aware of the pedestrian on the right to confirm they stay on the sidewalk.
5. [Accepted]
6. ✅ The bus ahead does not currently obstruct my path but remains within observation.
Error patterns in Sample 1

📋 Reasoning Review Guidelines
▾
🔗
Required structure: visible cue → ego vehicle response. ✗ The ego vehicle drives carefully near the junction. ✓ A pedestrian is crossing ahead, so the ego vehicle slows down and prepares to stop.
🚫
Generic phrases are never acceptable: ✗ "drive carefully" / "be cautious" / "stay alert" / "drive safely"
👁
The cue must be visually observable. No sensor references, no AI/system detection, no off-camera events.
🔮
No speculation about future events. ✗ The bus may stop to let passengers off. ✓ The bus does not currently obstruct my path.
🔄
If a fact was corrected, update any reasoning that referenced it. Wrong facts propagate into wrong reasoning.
